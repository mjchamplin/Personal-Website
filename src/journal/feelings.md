I will probably eventually (hopefully?) have a lot to say about my experience taking Ketamine (technically ​*Spravato*​, nasally-delivered esketamine, but functionally the same) but I don’t right now. I sort of get little vignettes that I can describe. 

A few weeks ago, at my 4th session, I had what felt at the moment like a *profound* experience. I think a purely medical description would be that I dissociated, which is sort of what Ketamine is supposed to make you do, but it felt at the time completely transformative and fundamentally inexplicable. 

I was wearing an eye mask for the first time, listening to the Johns Hopkins playlist (mostly classical), and things started off extremely mellow. I was floating in blackness, seeing colors (more on this later) and having generally weird daydreamy thoughts. And I just let myself drift. I felt myself melt into the chair, and just sort of stopped noticing any physical sensations in my body; in hindsight it felt like being in a sensory deprivation tank, though description tends to fall short. I was adrift. I don’t remember now what I was thinking about, imagining; I know I started off thinking about the desert, about being in Utah or Arizona where you feel truly alone, basking in a kind of solitude. During this phase, I would later remember, I just sort of forgot I was a person. It’s like I got so distracted by daydreaming that I forgot where I was, what I was doing, and ultimately who I was. It didn’t feel like amnesia, just...like my mind had drifted completely away. I was somewhere else, something else, someone else, but also nothing. And then, for whatever reason (probably the drugs hitting their peak, which tends to happen about an hour after I begin) something shifted. I remembered what I was doing, where I was...but also not somehow? There was a period of time between the two. Between being adrift and being in the room in an armchair wearing headphones and eating a jolly rancher — there was space between the two. Description falls short again, I was coming back into myself but I was also not; I was aware that this, whatever this is, is all I have ever been, all I will ever be, but then like there was *only* this. I felt the rush of cortisol and panic, and in that moment I felt something singular: like what was happening was the most significant thing that had ever happened to me, that everything was now different, that nothing could ever be the same, that I could never be me again. 

I opened my phone and Googled “can Ketamine break you” and though I don’t exactly remember doing this, I do remember feeling a kind of terror at the idea I would not be able to go back. That I was gone. But I was still me, but could not in any capacity imagine being the me that I am used to being. The most useful description, even though it still doesn’t feel quite right, is that it felt completely incomprehensible and impossible that I would be able to *fit* back into my body. 

I had the distinct feeling in that moment that something profound was happening, that I was seeing and understanding things in the moment that I would not be able to comprehend later when I was just normal me; I had, for the next couple of days, the distinct feeling that something had happened I could not remember because I do not have the capacity to remember it. Like seeing a color that doesn’t exist, like discovering a sense that humans don’t have and therefore couldn’t possibly explain. 

There was an element that was visual: when I took off the mask, sunlight streamed in the window and I remember the edges of my vision feeling like a shimmering picture frame; it was light, but it was more than light, incomprehensible light. 

It was the *feeling* of light. In what certainly feels like the most cliche description of all my descriptions, I had the overwhelming realization that feeling was all there is. That everything is ultimately processed into feelings. Feelings are the assembly language of the human psyche, and everything else runs on top of this. That my perception of sunlight is entirely complete even without my eyes, so long as I can *feel* the sunlight. That anything else is ancillary, that the feeling is the ultimate truth, the ultimate reality. 

And then, again, I felt expansive. It’s not that I felt that my size had changed, but rather...that I was everything. Here, more than ever, words fail to come close. I was absolutely everything, I was some great and singular thing. 

This feels like a four-dimensional understanding in my mind, but I can only remember the three-dimensional projection of it. I am the flat stick figure trying to imagine moving on an axis I cannot perceive. 

---

In the days since, I have struggled to remember and comprehend what happened. I’ve had several sessions since and they’ve been varying degrees of trippy, but I have not made it back. I have not been able to touch the infinite. 

In describing it to Kaytee this evening — and in fact the reason I’m writing about it now — I said that whatever it was, whatever I call it, however other people describe it, to me it felt like an *untethering.*

My perception untethered. 

I feel like whatever constraints exist on my consciousness...just loosened a bit. There is a little bit more space there now.
