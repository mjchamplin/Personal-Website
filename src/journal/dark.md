Twice now I’ve encountered the same piece of music during ketamine therapy. It’s on the John’s Hopkins Center’s psychedelic research [playlist](https://www.hopkinsmedicine.org/news/articles/2021/02/playlist-for-a-psychedelic-journey), which I often default to if I don’t know what else to listen to. It’s a long playlist (7\+ hours, timed for a psilocybin trip) and I generally put it on shuffle so I don’t know what music to expect — I’ve learned that finding patterns can take me out of the experience a little. It’s why Brian Eno’s *Music for Airports* was really great for ketamine precisely once, but now I recognize too many recurring melodies. 

The piece in question is the first movement of Polish composer Henryk Górecki's *Symphony No. 3*. 

The first time I heard it, I took a screenshot (as a way of saving a song without having to take out and use my phone, I simply reach down and hit the two buttons to save a screenshot of my Lock Screen). I did this twice, and later found I’d taken two screenshots about ten minutes apart of the same piece of music. I didn’t realize until later that it was the same. 

The second time I encountered the piece was yesterday. When the track started, I immediately recognized it from before (I took a screenshot to confirm it later). What felt like hours passed, and I wondered if I was still inside the same piece of music. It felt like it had to be the same piece; there had been no pause to indicate an ending, and I was aware of the music as a single, cavernous thing. I knew it had edges, but I was too far from them to perceive them. I took another screenshot. 

I would learn later that these two moments — which felt hours apart — were only separated by about nine minutes. I went places in those nine minutes. I understood the shape of the music during those nine minutes. I *met people inside myself* during those nine minutes.

But the most striking thing to me is that I do not recall what the music *sounds* like. I know that it’s a piece for orchestra and there’s a libretto in there somewhere, but that is all. I can’t hum a tune from it or even say much about its structure or composition. I’m sure if I heard it I’d recognize it, but I’m presently loving the idea that I have met this piece twice in what feels like a dark forest. I have been there but I could not tell you where it is or what it looks like. 

*I have only been there in the dark.*
